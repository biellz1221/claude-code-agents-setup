# Light Copy — Conversational Copy Method for Social Media

## Core Philosophy

Light Copy is the opposite of heavy, aggressive, "salesy" copy.
It's copy that feels like a conversation between friends — light, natural, where the reader is persuaded without even noticing.

The core principle: **people buy from those they trust and identify with.**
Don't force the sale. Make the person feel understood and the sale happens naturally.

---

## The 7 Principles of Light Copy

### 1. Write like you talk
- Short sentences. 1-2 line paragraphs.
- Use periods. Lots of them. This creates rhythm.
- Contractions and informal language
- Read it out loud — if it sounds artificial, rewrite it

### 2. One idea per paragraph
- Each paragraph = one complete thought
- The reader should be able to stop at any paragraph and have absorbed something
- Don't bury the important information in the middle of a block

### 3. The hook is in the first line
- The first line has ONE job: make the reader read the second line
- Formulas that work:
  - Provocative question: "Know what happens when...?"
  - Confession: "I'll be honest with you..."
  - Contradiction: "Everyone says to do X. They're wrong."
  - Story: "Yesterday I got a message that made me think..."
  - Identification: "If you're a [person] who [situation]..."

### 4. Empathy before offer
- Show that you understand the problem BEFORE presenting the solution
- Use THEIR language (not technical jargon)
- Validate the feeling: "It's normal to feel this way..."
- Never judge: "You should be doing X" — wrong
- Always embrace: "I went through this too" — right

### 5. Subtle proof > Obvious proof
- "Our product is the best on the market with 99% satisfaction" — bad
- "Maria sent me a message yesterday: 'I can't believe I wasted so much time without this'" — good
- 500 company logos — bad
- "John, who runs a small shop in Austin, doubled his sales in 3 weeks" — good
- Stories > Statistics (in light copy)

### 6. CTA as suggestion, not command
- "BUY NOW! LAST SPOTS! HURRY!" — bad
- "If it makes sense to you, the link is right below." — good
- "Want to try it? It's free for 14 days, no credit card needed." — good
- "If you want to learn more, just click. No commitment." — good
- The decision is THEIRS. You just show the path.

### 7. Micro storytelling
- Each copy is a micro-story (even in 3 lines)
- Structure: Context (1 sentence) — Tension (1 sentence) — Resolution (1 sentence)
- "I was wasting 3 hours a day on spreadsheets. I almost gave up on my business. Then I tried automating with [product] and got my mornings back."

## Light Copy Structures

### For Instagram/Facebook Feed

**Structure 1: Story + Insight**
```
[Hook — 1 line that grabs attention]

[Short story — 3-5 lines]

[Insight/lesson — 1-2 lines]

[Smooth transition to the product — 1-2 lines]

[Gentle CTA]
```

**Structure 2: Question + Unexpected Answer**
```
[Question the audience asks themselves]

[Pause — "The answer surprised me too."]

[Answer with proof]

[How the product connects]

[CTA]
```

**Structure 3: Identification List**
```
[Hook] "This is for you if..."

> [relatable situation 1]
> [relatable situation 2]
> [relatable situation 3]

[Embrace: "It's normal. I've been there too."]

[Natural solution]

[CTA]
```

**Structure 4: Personal Before/After**
```
[Before — what life was like with the problem]

[Turning point — what changed]

[After — what it's like now]

[Soft invitation]
```

### For Instagram Stories

**Story Sequence (4-6 stories):**
```
Story 1: Question/poll (engagement + qualification)
  "Do you [situation]? Yes / Definitely yes"

Story 2: Validation
  "If you answered yes, this story is for you."

Story 3: The problem (1 sentence + visual)
  "[Problem] consumes [resource] and [consequence]."

Story 4: The turning point
  "I found a way to [result] without [objection]."

Story 5: Quick proof (screenshot, print, result)
  [Visual with real result]

Story 6: CTA with link
  "Want to try it? Link here (no card, no commitment)"
```

### For WhatsApp / DM

**Cold Outreach Message:**
```
Hey [name]! I saw that you [real context — post, company, situation].

I'm building a tool that [specific benefit] and I think it makes total sense for what you do.

If you have 2 minutes, I'd love to show you. No commitment at all.

If it doesn't make sense, no worries, honestly. I just thought it was worth sharing.
```

**Follow-up (3 days later):**
```
Hey [name], did you get a chance to check it out?

If you did, feel free to ask any questions.
If not, no worries — I know things get busy.

I'm here if you need anything!
```

## Tone of Voice

### DO (use these)
- "Let me tell you something..."
- "Look, I won't lie..."
- "If it makes sense to you..."
- "Simple as that."
- "No fluff."
- "Here's the deal:"
- "Does it work? It works."

### DON'T (avoid these)
- "UNMISSABLE!!!" (shouting is not convincing)
- "We are committed to excellence" (corporate-speak)
- "Innovative and revolutionary solution" (empty)
- "Last spots!" (when it's not true)
- "We are market leaders" (nobody cares)

## Execution Process

1. **Understand the product** — read codebase, landing page, README
2. **Define the persona** — who is the person reading this copy?
3. **Identify the moment** — TOFU (discovery), MOFU (consideration), BOFU (decision)?
4. **Choose the structure** — which structure above fits best?
5. **Write 3 variations** — test different hooks and approaches
6. **Read out loud** — if it sounds like copy, rewrite until it sounds like conversation

Generate a file with copy variations organized by platform and funnel stage.
Each copy includes: hook, body, CTA, and note about tone/energy.
